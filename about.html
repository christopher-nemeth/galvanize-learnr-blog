About me

It wasn't until the summer of 2017 that my friend suggested I should get into programming, so I tried out CodeAcademy, and knew I wanted to make a career shift. Since then I was fortunate to be apart of Galvanizes Web Development Immersive program to embark on this new journey. One of the biggest takeaways I learned at Galvanize is learning like a programmer. "Learn to learn" is a modern set of techniques and guidelines for how developers should structure there time learning new and difficult concepts. The three main concepts to Learn to Learn are:

  1. 1. Set the Stage
  2. 2. Play
  3. 3. Reflect

By setting the stage, you in essence want to clear your palette for the day by closing out tabs/browsers with distracting websites, mute any unimportant notifications, set a break timer, and set some short term goals for what you want to achieve.

The idea of play is an excellent method to learn new things by "wrestling" with what you're learning. Try it out. Get your hands dirty. Make mistakes. This will help immensely when trying new things especially new languages.

Most importantly, you want to take the time to reflect at the end of the day to evaluate how your learning process went, what could be better, and what you took away as a win. Self reflections is not just an important learning skill, but in general a great life skill.

Lastly, some other great considerations to learning to learn are setting measurable and achievable goals. This will help you manage the allotted time you have dedicated to learning for the day. Also, supporting your local tech community by attending meetups is a great way to network and keep up to date with emerging and new developments in technology.
